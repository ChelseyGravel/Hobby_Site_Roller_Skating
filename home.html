Getting Started

As a Sport, roller skating includes:
Speed skating, Roller hockey, Roller derby,
Group skating, Aggressive inline skating,
Dance and figure skating.

Getting started with a new hobby is exciting! But it can be frustrating and confusing too. It’s hard knowing just what you need to get started and where to begin without getting overwhelmed. After finding information and getting started, what do you do next? Here you can find those answers!

There are a few important things you will want to know before getting started. When skating it is always important to wear protective padding and gear. Wearing knee pads and elbow pads add important protection to your joints, and really improve confidence when learning. Depending on the surface you’re skating on you may also choose to wear wrist guards and a helmet. You only get one body. Always protect it!

Skate quality really does matter and can drastically improve or hinder a skater ability to move and stop. Starting your new journey on gently used or less expensive skates is still a good idea. But take into consideration that you may not be able to skate well or comfortably. This is really just a good way to first decide if this is a hobby you would enjoy doing and it would be useful to invest a little money into skates that can drastically improve your ability. Good skates can be several hundreds of dollars, but spending that kind of money on a growing feet can be a difficult thing to part with and when shopping for used skates you can find a better quality skate for less money. But we will talk more about skates in another section.

FAQ

How do I stop?

There are many ways to stop. Here are 4: Push the toe stop out infront of you when skating forward, or drag it behind you when going backward. Other options include widing both feet at the same time, pussing the heel out towards the outside of the foot, or turning to one side and pusing one foot out infront making the top of a “T” with your extened foot.

How can you skate without falling?

Falling is apart of skating. Lean forward slightly keeping your keens having a slight bend to balance over you feet, knees, hips, and shoulders. When you feel uneasy with your balance drop as close to to ground as possible when you feel your self faling. It hurts less the closer you are to what you’re hitting. Learn how to fall correctly, is important to learn as well!

How do I roller skate backwards?

Make a “V” shape with your feet. Push your heels apart and back together, look over your shoulder. And drag your foot “infront” of yourself to stop (if you were facing the direction you are going it would be draging behind you like stoping going forward).

Are pretective pads really necessary?

It is always a good idea to protect yourself. Wearing padding can improve confidince, both in new and advanced skills. Preventing injuries before they occur is always a good idea. You only get one body, treat it well.

History

The idea for roller blades came before roller skates. Inline skates were created in the early 1700s when a Dutch person attached wooden spools to strips of wood and nailed them to his shoes. In 1863, an American developed the conventional rollerskate model, with the wheels positioned side by side, and it became the skate of choice.

Roller skating is defined as an activity or sport that is done by traveling across the top of a hard surface on roller skates. It is a recreational activity that is also a sport and a good way to get around.

Roller skating was used in theater and musical performances in the early 1800’s, roller skates were designed too be used to skate in a straight line and made turning very difficult. By the 1840’s waitresses were using roller skates to serve customers, and performers were using roller skates in operas and the ballet. This boost in the 1850’s created the first rise in popularity, this was typically referred to as a “craze” as rollerskating would see many rises and falls in popularity of their next 100 years. There were a lot of improvements over the next 15 years and in 1865, wheels now were rubber and the style was changed to a 4-wheel design making turning possible now too! In 1880 roller skating became very popular, we have speed skating and figure skating today from this time in history.

In 1931 Robert Bruce received a metal and a world record for roller skating non-stop. His record breaking time would be 61 hours and 36 minutes. The Golden Age of Roller Skating was right around the corner starting in the 1930s. Many skating rinks saw a boom in popularity and several were built across the United States, only to see a decline in popularity soon after in the 1950’s.

The days of disco music in the 1970s brought with it a new craze to do on skates as well.

Roller skating rose to popularity in the 1980’s, roller rinks were popping up everywhere aging and rollerblades were created. As a new skate design based on the original design, rollerblades replaced ice skates during the off season for hockey players to still practice year round. Skaters were enjoying this new design and street skating became popular among the public. Skates were being mass produced with children wanting rollerblades for their next birthday or Christmas gift. Teenagers enjoyed a night with their friend at the rink: having a soda, showing off their dance moves on the rink, skating in lines with their friends, and enjoying a couples skate with the crush of the week. In the 90’s there were some more improvements and a major design change reduced the average weight by almost 50%.

As time went on and technology improved entertaining children became more and more difficult, and less and less active. With more children choosing an indoor computer activity over skating at the rink. Roller rinks saw a drastic decline in popularity in the 21st century.

Rollerskating became popular aging with the covid-19 pandemic. The shut down forced people to start looking for new hobbies and ways to entertain themselves after they were over binge watching the tiger king on netflix and drinking everything in the house. New ways to spend the day and get through everything in the new world. Millennials turned back to a hobby enjoyed in the early days: roller skating.

As the spread of online content creators started publishing dances being done on roller skates and the boom of enforced stay at home times grew longer and longer, more and more reached for a pair for skates.

Types of Skates
Traditional Skates

Traditional skates, known as quads, described as having 4 wheels per skate. Quad skates have two wheels in the front and two in the back. And inline skates, have their wheels in a line from heel to toe.

Speed Skates

Speed skates are available in both quad and inline options. Increasing movement with a boot cut below the ankle bone and with very little padding to also increase the foot to boot rato. These skates are best used for timed events focusing on speed like lap skating events, sprints, and marathons. These are also a good option to use in the place of jam skates if features like a toe stop is important, Whereas Jam skates use a toe plug.

Jam Skates

Jam skates are different than other types of rollerskates. With a low-cut design and a dance plug in place of the toe stop, so no stops. They are lightweight for agile movement.

Rhythm Skates

Rhythm skates are also designed for dancing. The difference between a Rhythm skate and a Jam skate is in the boot design. A Rhythm skate boot is very similar to an Artistic skate, with a high ankle boot. But just like with jam skates, rhythm skates don’t have a toe stop either and feature a dance plug like a Jam skates.
