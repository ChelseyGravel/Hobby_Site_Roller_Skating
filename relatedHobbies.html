Ice Skating

Ice skating is a fun activity done in the winter months outdoors or indoors year round. Ice skating is done by wearing ice skates, a boot very similar to an Artistic skate with a sharpened metal blade running from toe to hill Instead of wheels for gliding across the top of the ice. When skating outdoors you may not always have access to the equipment for smooth ice. Natural ice can be rather bumpy, but nothing beats the outdoors. Balancing the blades underneath the middle of your skates, it is easy to see why this sport is easy to fall in love with. the beautiful gliding and swift slicing of a metal blade on cold ice is Undescribable. There’s nothing quite like feeling the refreshing cold on your skin when out on a sunny day. Wearing protective clothing can improve your time in the cold environment. Wearing extra padding and thick socks are also helpful tips to remember. Choosing skates that fit well and are the right size is important. Pushing off and the actual act of ice skating and figure skating are very similar to artistic Skating. Also similarly to Roller Skating using the same pads to protect your body is a good idea here too!

Skate Boarding

This is a very popular and widely known sport. When getting started you’ll need a board that is 30-31” long and around 8” wide. Skate boards are primarily used for doing tricks and skating outside or at a skate park. Skateboarders have 4 wheels, each of these has two weights inside just like a roller skate wheels. Learning how to stand and balance on the board can take time, but when done right this is a very fun hobby. Some of the first things to practice mastering are: stepping on and off the board, how and where to place your feet when riding, How to transition to push off, how to turn, stop, and fall.

Using protective padding might not be the coolest thing, but it will definitely protect your body from injuries that can cause permanent damage and make participating in the sport impossible or difficult.

Long boarding

Long boarding is similar to skateboarding but a longboard is bigger. Long boards are typically 37 - 40” long and around 10” wide. Unlike skateboards, Longboards are designed for a more of a cruiser type of ride and not really for jumping tricks, though there are plenty of trick opportunities and options when using a longboard. Long boards also have bigger wheels that are better for speed. When learning the longboard it’s important to practice on a flat surface.

Some of the skills it is important to practice include: stepping onto the board, standing on the board, pushing off and pushing a few times, stopping and falling properly (just like with skateboarding). It’s always important to remember to wear protective padding and practice safety when practicing any sport where injury can occur.
